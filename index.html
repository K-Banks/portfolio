Background in Coding

Hamilton Liquid Handling Platform

I have spent a year working with the Hamilton STAR liquid-handling platform, a highly customizable machine designed to automate common and repetitive laboratory procedures. These procedures can range from a simple transfer of liquid between tubes or prep common tests such as setup for RT-PCR or Next-Gen sequencing runs.

The software used, VENUSÂ®, is designed to offer simple or complex programming. The most basic programming involves a drag-and-drop style of referencing established commands and sub-methods with a flowchart to order steps. The complex programming allows manipulation of classes, variables, the inclusion of loops and arithmetic functions, as well as the ability to write custom sub-methods for use in multiple tests. I was tasked with fully automating an entirely new test developed by MDxHealth, my previous employer.

Unfortunately, the Hamilton platform and VENUS software are protected by copyright so there is a severe lack of resources and examples available online. Much of this is due to Hamilton offering commissioned custom programming from their in-house programmers with a much smaller emphasis on helping clients develop thier own talent.
